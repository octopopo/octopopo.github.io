About Po-Hsun Chen, or Potion Chain

Be careful, this potion hot hot hot hot.

A Potion Recipe

I am Po-Hsun Chen, from a lovely country called Taiwan. I am now a student in USC, studying Game Development. I plays lots of game, from the old school game on GameBoy like Pokémon Yellow, to the latest games like Red Dead Redemption 2. Personally, I think games are perfect media to bring people together. I have several highlights in my life with me and friends playing games. I want to bring similar experience to other people, so that is why I want to be a game developer.

A cup of water - How I met games.

I got my first game boy at the age of five. When I turned it on, I instanly dived into the wonderful virtual world. Since then, my life got deeply connected with games. For me, games are not just an pure entertainment, it's a bridge that connect me to firends. My life from fifth grade to ninth grade is full of games. I made some lifelong friends because we kept playing games everyday in my house. At some point, I feel like "hey, I want to make such a cool thing that can bring joy to everyone". So here I am, on my way to a game developer.

2 dragon eggs - An innovative, thirsty soul

I enjoy coding a lot, especially when it is related to games. I like to implement unique gameplays that surprise people. I enjoy everytime wathcing people playing my games with a big smile on their face as if they drank a magic potion. Aside from being a gameplay innovator, I am also a thirsty learner. Diving into a large code base and figuring out how it work is like learning how to cook a secret potion from a witch. I want to know where the magic come from. Additionally, I want to cast my own magic.

3 drops of tears - Playing games should be a right, not a privilege.

Most of the games nowadays are all using either controllers or keyboard-mouse as input. But for people that are not as lucky as us, it is a huge block between them and joy. My goal is to create a game that can be easily enjoyed by everyone. With the emergence of Reality Technologies, we have multiple choices to play. We can shake our bodies, rotate our eyes, or even move a string of muscles. There are all kinds of possiblities await. I am excited about the making next generation of games.

4 spoons of sugar - For the team!

I grew up playing in baseball teams and singing in choirs, so I know the importance of being a team player. I learned how to be play well in a industrail team from my intership with "Age of Learning". With people from different disciplines, I need to be adaptivet to keep my high performance. My key is to be active, I don't afraid to ask people questions. I think it's a good way to start establishing a connection. Once I know how to get along with my teammates. I can boost the team with my energy.

Voilà, a magic potion.
